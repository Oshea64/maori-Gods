The father and mother of Maori God mythology

At the heart of Maori mythology lies the creation story of Ranginui (Sky Father) and Papatūānuku (Earth Mother). According to this myth, in the beginning, the world existed in complete darkness, with Ranginui and Papatūānuku tightly embraced, forming a primordial cosmic union. Their children, the gods, lived between them in this confined space.

However, the children of Ranginui and Papatūānuku desired light and space. They yearned to experience the world and bring life to its potential. One by one, the gods attempted to separate their parents, each meeting resistance from Ranginui and Papatūānuku.

Finally, Tāne Mahuta, the god of forests and birds, succeeded in pushing Ranginui high into the heavens, creating the sky. Papatūānuku became the Earth, with her surface stretching wide and far. The children of Ranginui and Papatūānuku found their places within this new world, shaping various aspects of nature and humanity.
