Maori mythology

Maori mythology is the rich and intricate belief system of the indigenous Maori people of New Zealand. It encompasses a vast array of gods, goddesses, spirits, and ancestral beings, each playing a vital role in the Maori worldview. These myths and stories have been passed down through generations as an essential part of their cultural heritage, shaping their identity, values, and connection to the natural world.

Maori mythology holds deep significance in Maori culture as it provides explanations for the creation of the universe, the origins of humanity, and the relationships between gods, humans, and the natural world. The myths serve as a foundation for their spiritual beliefs, rituals, and customs, influencing various aspects of daily life, art, and storytelling.

In essence, the Maori gods are the bedrock of Maori culture, infusing it with a deep sense of spirituality, respect for the natural world, and pride in ancestral heritage. Their role in ceremonies, art, and storytelling keeps these beliefs alive, fostering a strong cultural identity and the transmission of wisdom from one generation to the next. The gods' enduring significance is a testament to the profound impact of Maori mythology on every aspect of their lives.
